What is this?

Most lifting programs record progress through the amount of weighted lifted each session.

This makes it difficult to keep track of one's aesthetic goals in a quantifiable manner.

Acquire Aesthetics is a site for lifters to document their aesthetic progress through body measurements.


Guidelines for taking body measurements:

The easiest and most effective method is to use a soft tape measure to record the girth or circumference of each body part.

There are 8 different body parts that are recommended to be measured regularly.

  1. Neck - Standing, wrap the tape measure around the Adam's apple.
  2. Shoulders - Standing, wrap the tape measure around the widest part of the shoulders.
  3. Biceps - Measure at its largest girth with the arms at the sides.
  4. Chest - Standing, measure with breath out above the nipple.
  5. Waist - Standing, measure at the navel.
  6. Hips - Measure at the largest point, where the glutes protrude.
  7. Thigh - Standing, measure at the largest girth, below the glutes.
  8. Calf - Seated if you are measuring yourself or standing if you have a partner, measure at its largest girth.

Here are some tips to make sure your measurements are consistent and accurate.

  1. Refrain from exercise before measuring - Blood rushing to certain muscle groups may distort your actual proportions.
  2. Relax your muscles - Different muscle groups will change in size differently when flexed.
  3. Do not stretch the tape - The tape should be pulled to where it is lying flat against the skin all the way around.
